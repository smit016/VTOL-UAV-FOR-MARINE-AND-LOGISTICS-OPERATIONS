This report presents the conceptual design and preliminary analysis of a hybrid Vertical
Take-Off and Landing (VTOL) Unmanned Aerial Vehicle (UAV) intended for transportation of
time-sensitive payloads in remote and infrastructure-limited environments. The UAV is
designed to carry a payload of 3–5 kg over a range of up to 100 km while maintaining high
operational flexibility, energy efficiency, and autonomous capability.
A hybrid VTOL configuration is selected to combine the advantages of vertical lift for take-off
and landing with efficient fixed-wing cruise performance. The design process follows a
systematic conceptual design methodology based on the approach proposed by Daniel
Raymer. Key design parameters such as weight estimation, thrust requirements, power
loading, and tail sizing are determined analytically. The design is further validated through
aerodynamic analysis (CFD).
