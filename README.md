# VTOL-UAV-FOR-MARINE-AND-LOGISTICS-OPERATIONS
Tri-motor VTOL UAV design and performance evaluation for offshore sampling and rugged terrain operations, with emphasis on stability, aerodynamic efficiency, and high-wind robustness.
VTOL UAV – Tri-Motor Configuration
Overview

This repository presents the design and performance evaluation of a tri-motor VTOL UAV (front twin + rear single configuration) developed for offshore water sampling and payload delivery in rough terrain environments.

The system is intended to operate under high wind gusts, sandy/dusty conditions, and uneven terrain, where conventional UAV configurations face stability and reliability limitations.

Mission Objectives

Enable autonomous water sample collection over freshwater bodies and offshore regions

Perform payload delivery in remote and inaccessible terrains

Ensure stable VTOL operation under high wind and turbulent conditions

Maintain robust performance in sand/dust-laden environments

Configuration Description

Type: VTOL UAV

Motor Layout: Tri-motor

2 × Front motors

1 × Rear motor

Design Intent:

Improved pitch stability and control authority

Enhanced thrust distribution efficiency

Reduced structural and system complexity compared to quad/hexa configurations

Design Methodology

The development approach focused on mission-driven engineering:

Configuration Selection

Trade-off analysis between conventional multirotor and tri-motor layout

Selection based on weight, control, and mission suitability

Aerodynamic Considerations

Estimation of lift, drag, and thrust requirements

Analysis of propulsion efficiency under varying conditions

Stability & Control

Evaluation of center of gravity (CG) placement

Assessment of pitch and yaw control behavior in asymmetric configuration

Environmental Constraints

High wind gust tolerance

Operation in sand/dust-heavy regions

Rough terrain landing considerations

Performance Parameters Evaluated

Thrust-to-weight ratio

Payload carrying capacity

Stability under gust conditions

Power requirements and endurance (estimated)

Operational feasibility in harsh environments

Applications

Environmental monitoring and water quality analysis

Coastal and offshore data collection

Logistics and last-mile delivery in remote regions

Disaster response in inaccessible areas

Key Highlights

Non-conventional tri-motor VTOL configuration

Mission-oriented design for extreme environmental conditions

Focus on practical deployment scenarios, not just theoretical modeling

Future Work

CFD-based aerodynamic validation

Prototype development and real-world testing
